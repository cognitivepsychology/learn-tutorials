When you embed a Plotly graph, it means you’re sharing your graph, data and the code that describes your graph all in one place. With our interactive features, your readers have a whole new way to engage with your work. When you update your Plotly graph, the graph automatically updates on your blog or website. No need for manual updates! Streaming data is also supported in embedded graphs. Learn more about how to interface Plotly with your favorite hardware in the Plotly Workshop .

Plotly’s interactive charts add impact to your blog, webpage, online notebook, IPython notebook and lesson plans: we’re in Wired Magazine , The Washington Post , and the National Post.

The best way to embed a Plotly graph is with iFrames .  You’ll just need to paste a snippet of HTML code into your webpage or blog.  In this tutorial, we’ll show you how to get the right code.  We’ve included tips on how to place the code in your website in Step 2 below.  Finally, if you’re using wordpress.com, jump to the explanation below. Wordpress doesn’t support iFrames, but we’ve come up with a workaround for you.

Step 1: Get the HTML snippet.

There are two places to locate the embed code in Plotly.  If you want to share someone else’s graph, Option 1, is the easier choice.  If you’re in your workspace, then you’ll want to check out Option 2.

Option 1: If the graph you’re looking at is displayed in the format seen on the right, then Option 1 is for you.

Notice the sharing options located in the bottom right corner.  You want to click on the button </> .

Option 2: In your workspace, you’ll see an option to Share in the top right of your browser window.  Click it.

In the sharing box that opens, click on the Embed</> button .  You’ll need to make sure that the graph is set to “public” in order to generate the HTML code.

Choose the embed size you want, then copy the HTML (the code may be in the bottom right corner of the embed window). Paste the code into your Tumblr, website, or blog.

An alternative is to copy and paste the snippet below.  You’ll have to replace the URL of our graph with your own.

<iframe width="640" height="480" frameborder="0" seamless="seamless" scrolling="no" src="https://plot.ly/~Dreamshot/411/.embed?width=640&height=480" ></iframe>

Step 2: Paste the HTML snippet into your site’s code.

Paste the iFrame HTML code into your site’s source code, and resize as needed.  At Plotly, we use tumblr for our blog . Wordpress workaround.

Wordpress.com doesn’t support iFrames. Here’s how you can download an image of your graph to embed in your Wordpress site. The image is static, but you can link back to your interactive graph hosted at Plotly. If you’re hosting your own site and using wordpress.org, there are a few options for plugins that will help you with iFrames.

Plotly allows you to download a copy of any graph from your workspace.  Once you have a graph loaded, you will see a Download button.

The “Download/Export” box let’s you pick a file type and download an image of your graph. The file you download won’t be interactive, but you will still be able to link to the interactive version of your chart hosted on Plotly.

Notice the “Include public link” box. Check this box so that your graph's URL is written on your graph.
